# How Many Children Do American Wars Kill?

On Feb. 28, 2026, an American airstrike killed 165 school girls at the Shajaraeh Tayyebeh school in the city of Minab in Iran. This marked the beginning of a war which has impacted global oil trade, killed Iran's supreme leader Ayatollah Ali Khamenei, and dragged the entire region into war. UNICEF released a statement on the same day, marking it "a dangerous moment for millions of children in the region."

https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2026/3/3/questions-over-minab-girls-school-strike-as-israel-us-deny-involvement 

https://www.unicef.org/mena/press-releases/unicef-statement-impact-military-escalation-children-middle-east 

A few weeks later, Amnesty International published a report calling for accountability against those involved in the planning and execution of the "unlawful US strike on a school in Minab, Hormozgan."

https://www.amnesty.org.au/iran-those-responsible-for-deadly-us-strike-on-school-must-be-held-accountable/

As several observers called it an "unprecedented" attack on children, I realized it might be a task worth undertaking to look at how many children U.S. airstrikes and drone strikes had killed in the first 20 days of a war that the country initiated or participated in.

Based on secondary source research, I compiled a list of wars the U.S. has directly participated in or indirectly facilitated by providing military and monetary aid in the past 20 years.

- Iraq War (2003-2011)
- Afghanistan (2001-2021)
- Libya (2011)
- Syria (2014-2024)
- Pakistan Drones (2004-2018)
- Israel-Gaza (2023-present)
- Israel-Lebanon (2006-present)
- Israel-Iran (2025)
- Israel-Iran (2026)
- Ukraine (2022-present)
- KSA/UAE-Yemen (2015-present)

To normalize the data, I chose to compare only the first 20 days of each war. I counted every person killed under the age of 14 as a child.

## Data

I compiled the data for each war by hand, and decided to include the following data points for each war:


`Conflict_name` | How the specific war was referred to in public discourse |
`Operation_name` | The official name for the military operation assigned by official military organizations |
`Conflict_start_date` | The start date for the conflict |
`Time_period` | The duration of the conflict |
`Date_recorded` | The date on which these figures were recorded |
`Children_killed_min_first_20_days` | The minimum number of children killed in the first 20 days of the war |
`Children_killed_max_first_20_days` | The maximum number of children killed in the first 20 days of the war |
`Perpetrators` | Member states of the American coalition |
`Source` | Sources of information for all data points |

## Data Decisions

- Since there were too many incidents in the "Iraqi Freedom" operation where the exact demographic information was not specified, I only selected children in cases where it was specifically mentioned that the child was under 14 years of age.
- For cases where the date range spanned over several days, I counted every incident where the range included at least one bound of the 20-day range in focus for this data collection.
- One incident from the Iraq Body Counts database cited up to 2,000 deaths without any demographic information and so was not included in the number of children killed in the first 20 days of the war.
https://www.iraqbodycount.org/database 
- For any wars before 2010, daily figures are difficult to track down. These numbers are in the process of improvement.
- In the case of Afghanistan, UNAMA was not set up until 2007 to conduct daily documentation. So for the first 20 days of the war, I picked the three most widely-reported incidents: Karam village on Oct 11, Kabul neighborhoods on Oct 13, and Chowkar-Karez on Oct 21 and 22, 2001. In 2001, approximately 49.9% of Afghanistan's population was under the age of 15; for these incidents I have taken 40% of civilian casualties to represent children.
https://www.macrotrends.net/global-metrics/countries/afg/afghanistan/population
- It is worth reiterating, these estimates are ONLY for the first 20 days of war. In most cases, a lot of children were killed over the course of the many months of war.
- In the case of Syria, Operation Inherent Resolve was authorized officially a few days before the first official strikes, so it includes one report from Radio Free Syria which goes beyond the 20-day bound.
- The most depressing decision was whether to keep the Gaza count, because I really wanted to focus on how killing 168 children in Minab in one day is truly unprecedented. In just 20 days, Israel killed more than 3,000 Palestinian children.
