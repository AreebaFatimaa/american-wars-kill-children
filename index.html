American wars kill children, but the Minab airstrike is still unprecedented

American airstrike in Iran killed more children in the first 20 day than any U.S. war in 20 years

By Areeba Fatima

On Feb. 28, 2026, an American airstrike killed 165 school girls at the Shajaraeh Tayyebeh school in the city of Minab in Iran.

This marked the beginning of a war which has impacted global oil trade, killed Iran's supreme leader Ayatollah Ali Khamenei, and dragged the entire region into war. UNICEF released a statement on the same day, marking it "a dangerous moment for millions of children in the region."

A few weeks later, Amnesty International published a report calling for accountability against those involved in the planning and execution of the “unlawful US strike on a school in Minab, Hormozgan.”

As several observers called it an “unprecedented” attack on children, it is worth comparing deaths of children across U.S. airstrikes and drone strikes in the first 20 days of a war that the country initiated or participated in.
